Ras Malai

Ras Malai is a popular and indulgent Indian dessert, often enjoyed at special occasions, festivals, and celebrations. It consists of soft, spongy white cream-soaked dumplings made from chhena (a form of fresh Indian cheese), soaked in flavored milk. Ras Malai is known for its rich, creamy texture and fragrant, aromatic flavors, making it a beloved dessert in South Asia, particularly in India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, and Nepal.








Flavor Profile
Creamy and Sweet: Ras malai is rich and decadent, with the soft chhena dumplings soaking in sweet, flavored milk. The milk is infused with the fragrance of cardamom, saffron, and sometimes rose water. Aromatic: The combination of cardamom, saffron, and occasionally rose water or kewra gives ras malai a fragrant, almost floral essence. Soft and Spongy: The chhena dumplings are soft, light, and spongy, soaking up the milk syrup, making them melt in the mouth.
