Assam Tea



Assam tea Growing Region

Assam is located along the Brahmaputra River in northeastern India. The region's climate is hot and humid, with heavy rainfall and rich alluvial soil, ideal for cultivating tea. The tea gardens are mostly located at altitudes ranging from 300 to 800 meters above sea level, although higher elevations produce a slightly lighter flavor, Assam Orthodox Tea: Traditionally made using the orthodox method, where leaves are hand-rolled, giving a more complex flavor. Assam CTC (Crush, Tear, Curl): A more modern method that produces small, hard pellets of tea leaves, often used for mass-market tea bags. It brews quickly and has a stronger flavor, often used in blends or for making chai.
