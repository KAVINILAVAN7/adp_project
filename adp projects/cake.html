Cashew Cakes




Main Ingredients

Cashews: The star ingredient, cashews can be used in different forms: whole, chopped, or ground. They can be roasted or raw, depending on the recipe, and contribute to the cake's texture and flavor. Flour: Most cashew cakes are made with all-purpose flour or a mix of flours. In some recipes, almond flour or other gluten-free alternatives can be used. Sugar: Typically granulated sugar or sometimes powdered sugar is used to sweeten the cake. Butter/Oil: A key ingredient for the cake's richness, butter or oil helps to achieve a moist, tender crumb. Eggs: Eggs are commonly used to bind the ingredients together and help with the cake's rise. Baking Powder/Soda: These are used to make the cake rise and give it a light, airy texture. Milk or Cream: Liquid ingredients like milk or cream are added for moisture and to create the right consistency of the batter. Flavorings: Vanilla extract, cardamom, cinnamon, or other spices can be added for extra flavor.


Health Benefits

High in Protein and Healthy Fats: Cashews are a good source of plant-based protein, monounsaturated fats, and essential minerals like magnesium, phosphorus, and copper. Rich in Antioxidants: Cashews contain antioxidants like vitamins E and K, which can help protect the body from oxidative stress. Heart-Healthy: The healthy fats in cashews may support heart health, lowering bad cholesterol levels.
